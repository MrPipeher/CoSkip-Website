Important Limitations

Co-Skip uses advanced techniques for compatibility, but some limitations exist, especially against highly secure proctoring software:

  • Screenshots (Ctrl+J) May Fail: Environments like Lockdown Browser are *designed* to block screen capture. If screenshots don't work, **use text input (Ctrl+L)**, which is the primary reliable method.
  • Hotkey Conflicts / Blocking: Default hotkeys (like Ctrl+M) might conflict with other running applications or be blocked by specific lockdown configurations. Use the Settings hotkey (Ctrl+Shift+S initially, or your customized key) to choose different key combinations if the defaults don't work. In rare cases with extreme security settings (often institution-specific deep kernel filters), *all* hotkey combinations might be blocked, or your quiz/exam might only permit multiple-choice input.
  • Camera Visibility: We hide the app digitally, but proctoring cameras can still see you physically typing. Be mindful of appearances.
  • Responsible Use:** Adhere to all academic integrity policies and terms of service. Co-Skip is a powerful tool; use it ethically for legitimate assistance.

Even with these caveats, Co-Skip offers a unique and effective way to get text-based assistance when other methods are blocked.
